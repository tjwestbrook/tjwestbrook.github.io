Sudoku Artificial Intelligence & Algorithm Efficiency · 2016

Through the Advanced Research Mentorship (ARM) program at Glastonbury High School, I worked with Laurent Michel, Ph.D., in the Department of Computer Science and Engienering at the University of Connecticut. By researching Crook's algorithm and the brute-force algorithm, I was able to write a Sudoku-solving program in C++ while utilizing recursive and oject-oriented programming.

Puzzle Pride: An LGBTQ+ Educational Game · 2015

Hosted by the MassMutual Life Insurance company, I completed a six-week program devoted to encouraging students to pursue computer science at the collegiate level and was educated in robotics, graphics and animation, data structures, algorithms, and mobile app development. I developed a web application utilizing Processing.js and object-oriented programming to create a tile-swapping and reimaging game that educates users on LGBT communities and their pride flags.

Atari Pong / Table Tennis:
Dynamics & Mutlimedia Aspects of Game Design · 2014

Under the guidance of Theodore Raphan, Ph.D., in the Department of Computer and Information Science at Brooklyn College through a STEM program hosted through New York University, I researched the dynamics and multimedia aspects of game design. With object-oriented programming and the use of Processing.js, I re-created the Atari Pong game, also known as table tennis.
